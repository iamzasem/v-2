A guide to declining adjectives without articles in German (strong declension), with examples, exercises, and resources.

Understanding Adjective Declension without Articles

What Is Strong Declension?

When no article (e.g., der, ein) precedes the noun, the adjective takes "strong" endings to show gender, number, and case.

  • Example: Frischer Fisch (fresh fish, masculine nominative) - -er marks masculine and nominative.
