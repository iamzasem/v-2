"Aus + Material" in German

A guide to using aus with materials in the dative case to describe what something is made of, with examples, exercises, and resources.

What Is "Aus + Material"?

The preposition aus paired with a material in the dative case indicates the substance an object is composed of, answering "What is it made of?"

Key Points

  • Dative Case: Materials follow aus in dative (e.g., Holz → aus Holz).
  • No Article: General materials omit articles (e.g., aus Metall).
  • Specificity: Definite articles specify a particular material (e.g., aus dem Glas).

Structure and Formation

Formation

Aus + material noun in dative, with or without an article depending on generality.

Uses of "Aus + Material"

Basic: Describing Objects

  1. Ein Stuhl aus Holz (A chair made of wood)

Intermediate: Craftsmanship

  1. Eine Vase aus Glas (A vase made of glass)

Advanced: Everyday Items

  1. Ein Kleid aus Stoff (A dress made of fabric)

Tips to Master "Aus + Material"

  • Use Dative: Always apply the dative case after aus (e.g., aus Holz).
  • Skip Articles: Omit articles for general materials (e.g., aus Glas, not aus dem Glas).
  • Context Matters: Use aus for composition, not decoration (e.g., aus Gold, not mit Gold).
  • Practice Materials: Memorize common ones like Holz, Metall, Glas.
