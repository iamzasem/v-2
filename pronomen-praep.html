What Are Pronouns with Prepositions?

Pronouns pair with prepositions to replace nouns, taking accusative, dative, or genitive cases for people, while pronominal adverbs (da-, wo-) replace inanimate objects.

Key Points

  • Pronouns: For people (e.g., mit ihr - with her).
  • Pronominal Adverbs: For things (e.g., damit - with it).
  • Cases: Determined by the preposition (e.g., f√ºr + accusative).
