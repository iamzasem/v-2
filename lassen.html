Master the German Verb "Lassen": A B1 Guide to Meaning and Usage

If you’re learning German at the B1 level, the German verb "lassen" is a must-know! This versatile verb means "to let" or "to allow" and is essential for improving your German grammar skills. Whether you’re exploring the "lassen meaning" or learning how to use lassen, this guide offers clear explanations, 25 examples, exercises, and resources to help you master it for your B1 journey!

What Does "Lassen" Mean in German?

The German verb ''"lassen"'' primarily means "to let" or "to allow," but its uses extend far beyond that for B1 learners. It’s an irregular verb (stem: 'lass-', past: 'ließ', participle: 'gelassen') and serves as a main verb, a modal-like verb, or part of expressions. Mastering "lassen" is key to learning German grammar effectively.

Key Points

  • Basic Meaning: "To let" or "to leave" something as it is.
  • Modal Use: Combines with an infinitive to mean "to have something done" or "to cause something."
  • Reflexive Use: With 'sich', it means "to be possible" or "to let oneself."
  • Expressions: Includes idioms like 'sich nicht lumpen lassen' (to go all out).

Top Uses of "Lassen" for B1 Learners

To Let or Allow

  1. Meine Mutter lässt mich ins Kino gehen. (My mom lets me go to the cinema.)

To Leave Something

  1. Ich lasse mein Buch auf dem Tisch. (I leave my book on the table.)

Causative Use (Have Something Done)

  1. Er lässt sein Auto reparieren. (He has his car repaired.)

Modal-Like Use (Let It Happen)

  1. Sie lässt die Kinder spielen. (She lets the kids play.)

Reflexive (Sich Lassen)

  1. Das lässt sich machen. (That can be done.)

How to Use "Lassen" Like a Native

  1. Meine Eltern lassen mich am Wochenende ausgehen. (My parents let me go out on the weekend.)
  2. Er lässt sein Zimmer immer aufräumen. (He always has his room tidied up.)
  3. Das Problem lässt sich leicht lösen. (The problem can be easily solved.)
  4. Ich lasse die Tasche im Auto. (I leave the bag in the car.)
  5. Wir lassen uns nicht beeinflussen. (We don’t let ourselves be influenced.)

Word Order: With an infinitive, both verbs go to the end (e.g., 'Ich lasse es machen.' - I have it done.)

Tenses: Conjugate normally (e.g., 'Ich ließ ihn gehen.' - I let him go.)

Negation: Add 'nicht' before the infinitive or after "lassen" (e.g., 'Ich lasse ihn nicht warten.' - I don’t let him wait.)

Exercises to Practice "Lassen" for B1 Learners

Exercise 1: Fill in the Blank

Choose the correct form of "lassen" (e.g., lasse, lässt, ließ, lassen) in nuanced contexts.

  1. Ich ___ meinen Regenschirm im Café.
  2. Er ___ die Katze über den Zaun springen.
  3. Letzte Woche ___ sie ihren Gast lange warten.
  4. Wir ___ die Musik laut spielen.
  5. Sie ___ ihre Schuhe polieren.

Answers:

  1. lasse (I leave my umbrella in the café.)
  2. lässt (He lets the cat jump over the fence.)
  3. ließ (Last week she let her guest wait for a long time.)
  4. lassen (We let the music play loudly.)
  5. lässt (She has her shoes polished.)

Exercise 2: Translate into German

Translate these nuanced sentences using "lassen" with precise grammar and word order.

  1. I let him decide the menu.
  2. She leaves her sunglasses on the balcony.
  3. We had the piano tuned.
  4. That lets itself be arranged.
  5. He doesn’t let me explain the situation.

Answers:

  1. Ich lasse ihn das Menü entscheiden.
  2. Sie lässt ihre Sonnenbrille auf dem Balkon.
  3. Wir lassen das Klavier stimmen.
  4. Das lässt sich arrangieren.
  5. Er lässt mich die Situation nicht erklären.

Exercise 3: Rewrite Sentences

Rewrite these nuanced sentences using "lassen" in a causative sense with proper grammar.

  1. Ich wasche meine Jacke.
  2. Sie bügelt die Vorhänge.
  3. Wir richten den Garten ein.
  4. Er restauriert das Gemälde.
  5. Ich nähe mein Kleid.

Answers:

  1. Ich lasse meine Jacke waschen. (I have my jacket washed.)
  2. Sie lässt die Vorhänge bügeln. (She has the curtains ironed.)
  3. Wir lassen den Garten einrichten. (We have the garden set up.)
  4. Er lässt das Gemälde restaurieren. (He has the painting restored.)
  5. Ich lasse mein Kleid nähen. (I have my dress sewn.)

Exercise 4: Match the Meaning

Match the nuanced sentence to its correct English translation, focusing on idiomatic uses.

  1. Ich lasse den Kuchen stehen.
  2. Er lässt mich antreten.
  3. Das lässt sich besprechen.
  4. Wir lassen das Licht brennen.
  5. Sie lässt die Pflanzen verdorren.

Answers:

  1. c) I leave the cake standing.
  2. b) He lets me line up.
  3. a) That can be discussed.
  4. c) We leave the light burning.
  5. a) She lets the plants wither.

Exercise 5: Create Your Own Sentences

Write original, nuanced sentences using "lassen" based on these prompts with proper grammar.

  1. Let someone do something unusual.
  2. Leave something in an unexpected place.
  3. Have something done professionally.
  4. Something is possible to negotiate.
  5. Don’t let something happen intentionally.

Answers:

  1. Ich lasse meinen Nachbarn jonglieren. (I let my neighbor juggle.)
  2. Ich lasse meine Brille im Kühlschrank. (I leave my glasses in the fridge.)
  3. Er lässt seine Uhr reparieren. (He has his watch repaired.)
  4. Das lässt sich verhandeln. (That can be negotiated.)
  5. Wir lassen das Team nicht gewinnen. (We don’t let the team win.)
